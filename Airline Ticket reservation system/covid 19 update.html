Latest Updates On Covid-19




For Your Safety....

  1. Stay informed: Stay updated on COVID-19 guidelines and travel restrictions of your destination country. Check official sources such as the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) or the World Health Organization (WHO) for the latest information.
  2. Get vaccinated: If you are eligible, get vaccinated against COVID-19 before traveling. Vaccination reduces the risk of severe illness and hospitalization. Follow the vaccination guidelines of your home country and destination country.
  3. Wear masks: Wear masks in public settings, particularly indoors or when social distancing is not possible. Use masks that cover both your nose and mouth properly.
  4. Practice good hand hygiene: Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. If soap and water are not available, use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.
  5. Maintain social distancing: Keep a distance of at least 1 meter (3 feet) from others, especially in crowded places. Avoid close contact with people who are sick or displaying COVID-19 symptoms.
  6. Avoid crowded areas: Stay away from crowded places, both indoors and outdoors. Choose outdoor activities over indoor activities whenever possible.
  7. Follow local regulations: Adhere to the local COVID-19 regulations, including mask mandates, curfews, and social distancing measures. Respect the local customs and guidelines to protect yourself and others.
  8. Monitor your health: Be aware of COVID-19 symptoms such as fever, cough, difficulty breathing, loss of taste or smell, and fatigue. If you experience any symptoms, seek medical advice and follow the local health protocols.
  9. Travel insurance: Consider purchasing travel insurance that covers COVID-19-related expenses, including medical treatment, trip cancellations, or interruptions.
  10. Be prepared: Carry a sufficient supply of masks, hand sanitizers, disinfectant wipes, and other personal hygiene items. Familiarize yourself with the healthcare facilities and emergency contact numbers of your destination.
  11. Follow quarantine requirements: Some countries may have mandatory quarantine or self-isolation requirements upon arrival. Make sure to understand and comply with these regulations.
    12. Remember that the COVID-19 situation can change rapidly, so it's important to stay flexible and adapt your plans accordingly. Stay updated with the latest travel advisories and guidelines from health authorities to ensure your safety while abroad.
