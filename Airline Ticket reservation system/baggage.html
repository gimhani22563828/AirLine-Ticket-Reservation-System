Baggage Allowance



  1. Baggage allowances typically vary based on the airline, travel class, and fare type.
  2. Most airlines have specific weight and size restrictions for both checked baggage and carry-on baggage.
  3. Checked baggage refers to the luggage that is stored in the cargo hold of the aircraft, while carry-on baggage is the smaller bag or suitcase that passengers can bring into the cabin.
  4. The number of checked bags allowed may vary, usually ranging from 1 to 3 bags, depending on the airline, travel class, and destination.
  5. The maximum weight per checked bag is typically around 20 kg to 32 kg (44 lbs to 70 lbs), again depending on the airline and travel class.



  1. Carry-on baggage allowances usually include restrictions on size and weight. Passengers are typically allowed to bring one personal item (such as a purse or laptop bag) and one carry-on bag.
  2. The maximum weight for carry-on bags is usually around 7 kg to 10 kg (15 lbs to 22 lbs), and there are specific size limits to ensure the bag fits in the overhead compartment or under the seat in front of you.
  3. Some airlines offer different baggage allowances for different fare types, such as economy, premium economy, business, and first class.
  4. It's important to note that excess baggage beyond the allowed limits may incur additional fees, and restrictions may apply to certain items such as fragile or dangerous goods.
  5. Always check the specific airline's website or contact their customer service for the most accurate and up-to-date information on baggage allowances before your flight.
